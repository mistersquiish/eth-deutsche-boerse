Stockchain

Problem

The current listing process at Deutsche Boerse (DB) is cumbersome and lengthy which urgently needs a makeover. For a stock to be listed on the exchange, the issuer must complete a form which contains all the required information, sign it by hand, scan it and send it to the listing department. Then, the listing person reviews the information for its accuracy and checks if any question is to be raised for the stock. If anything is in doubt, an inquiry is started and the issuer must update the information. This is usually caused by asymmetric information stored in different databases of issuers and the stock exchange.

Blockchain

Blockchain would solve four major problems with the current listing process:
  • Solves transparency
  • Synchronises databases
  • Faster processing times
  • Digital cryptography for signature

Solution

  1. Company submits their financials to a bank
  2. Bank reviews information and submits listing to the blockchain
  3. Other banks can scrutinize the listing and choose to approve
  4. Once all banks approve, the listing is officially approved and ready for the stock exchange
Notes: Only users who hold a verified bank's private key can make approvals

Challenges

The biggest challenge posed is adopting blockchain technology. Acceptance and regulation by the different governing bodies of the exchanges will be the main deterrent. Legal regulations regarding the capital and derivative markets are numerous. There may be complex legal issues regarding handling confidential information of companies. In addition, blockchain itself is complex and requires heavy subject matter expertise to be implemented. Exchanges would have to invest much more in IT infrastructure in addition to hiring blockchain experienced developers.
